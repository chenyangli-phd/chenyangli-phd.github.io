About Me

Available for work

Numerical Methods and Finite Element Analysis. My research is centered on the numerical analysis of partial differential equations arising in fluid dynamics and multi-physics systems, with a strong emphasis on finite element methods. In particular, I focus on stabilized mixed finite element formulations for incompressible flows. My work addresses key numerical issues such as stability, well-posedness and optimal error estimates, with special attention to time-dependent problems.

Variable-Density and Complex Fluid Models. I also work on time-dependent coupled models with variable density, where the loss of skew-symmetry and additional nonlinear transport effects pose significant analytical and numerical challenges. The models under investigation include variable-density Navier–Stokes equations, natural convection models with density variations, variable-density MHD systems, and the variable-density Ericksen–Leslie system for nematic liquid crystals.

Coupled Time-Dependent Multi-Physics Systems. A major component of my research concerns the development and analysis of finite element schemes for time-dependent coupled systems, including the Navier–Stokes equations, Stokes–Darcy systems, natural convection models, magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) systems, and chemotaxis–Navier–Stokes models. These systems are characterized by strong nonlinear coupling and multiple interacting fields.
