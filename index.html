I am a Ph.D. student in Computational Mathematics at the School of Mathematical Sciences, East China Normal University. My research focuses on numerical methods for partial differential equations, particularly the finite element method and its applications to coupled multi-physics problems. In addition to my academic pursuits, I have a strong interest in photography. Photography allows me to observe the world from different perspectives and fuels my creativity in both science and life.

Available for work

Primary Research Areas: Computational fluid dynamics, Numerical solution of partial diﬀerential equations (PDEs), Finite element methods, Stabilized mixed finite element methods.

Analysis of Coupled Systems: Numerical analysis and simulation of the time-dependent coupling model including Navier-Stokes equation, Stokes-Darcy System, Natural Convection Model, Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) System, Chemotaxis–Navier-Stokes System.

Models with Variable Density: The time-dependent coupling model with variable density including Navier-Stokes Equation with variable density, Natural Convection Model with variable density, Magnetohydrodynamics System with variable density, Ericksen-Leslie system with variable density.
